Automated Surface-to-Solid Conversion for Bio-Printable Patch Fabrication from Three-Dimensional Wound Surface Models

2026

Authors: Nguyen, P.T.A., Tran, C., Tran, H.D.T., Pham, T.T.H., et al.

Accepted at The 11th International Conference on Biomedical Engineering in Vietnam (BME11) .

This work presents an automated pipeline for converting reconstructed wound surface models into fabrication-ready bio-printable patches. The framework integrates wound segmentation, 3D reconstruction, mesh processing, and conformal patch generation for personalized wound treatment applications.

Key results include:

  • Dice Score: 0.921 ± 0.047
  • Mean Chamfer Distance: 2.639 mm
  • Successful processing of 21/21 wound cases
  • 23.3% reduction in bio-ink consumption compared with conventional rectangular patches

Accepted for presentation and publication at BME11.

Stress Recognition from Wearable Signals

Problem: Stress is a leading driver of burnout and chronic illness, but existing detection tools are either clinical or unreliable in real-world conditions.

Solution: Developed a CNN-LSTM framework combined with Double Deep Q-Networks (DDQN) and Active Learning for stress recognition from wearable physiological signals. Evaluated using Leave-One-Subject-Out (LOSO) validation and achieved 95.1% accuracy with strong cross-subject generalization.

Automated Bio-Printable Wound Patch Generation

Problem: Manual or semi-automated wound patch design from 3D wound surfaces is time-consuming, inconsistent, and often results in inefficient material usage and non-conformal patches that do not properly fit complex wound geometries.

Solution: 4-stage automated pipeline — OBB-guided cropping → U-Net segmentation (Dice 0.921) → COLMAP dense reconstruction (CD 2.639 mm) → conformal STL patch generation. Achieves 23.3% bio-ink savings vs standard rectangular patches, watertight solids for 21/21 cases.

Publication: Accepted at the 11th International Conference on Biomedical Engineering in Vietnam (BME11).
